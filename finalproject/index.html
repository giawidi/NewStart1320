About Chattanooga

Chattanooga, a city in southeastern Tennessee, is set along the Tennessee River in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains. Its trolleylike Incline Railway scales steep Lookout Mountain before reaching Ruby Falls waterfall and Rock City, featuring sweeping views, sandstone formations and gardens. Point Park, also atop Lookout, marks the site of a Civil War battle now honored at the Battles for Chattanooga Museum.

Quick Facts

  • Chattanooga is in southeast Tennessee along the Tennessee River.
  • It's between the Cumberland Plateau and the ridge-and-valley Appalachians.
  • The name "Chattanooga" comes from a Creek Indian word meaning "rock coming to a point".
  • The city was incorporated in 1839.
  • Known as the "Scenic City" due to its stunning mountain views and riverfront.
  • Recognized for its sustainability efforts, including hydroelectric power from the nearby Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA) and solar energy projects.
  • Offers a wide range of activities, including hiking Lookout Mountain, exploring the Tennessee Aquarium, rock climbing, and kayaking on the Tennessee River.
  • Chattanooga has a growing arts scene, with murals, galleries, and music festivals, plus a booming food and craft beer culture.
